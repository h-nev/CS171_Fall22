DOLLY PARTON

Dolly Parton was born in the impoverished Appalachian region of Tennessee in 1946, growing up with 12 siblings. Her rise to fame is undeniably impressive, landing her in many halls of fame, associated with hundreds of songs, receiving over one hundred awards, and donating a vast majority of her wealth to support her community.

Ranging from classic country legend Johnny Cash to pop country hit Luke Bryan, country music casts a wide net. Still, it's sound is unique to the United States. A bubble that doesn't stand out in the presence of Taylor Swift and Elvis Presley is renowned artist Dolly Parton.

She has received awards notably from the Sevierville Chamber of Commerce, Hollywood Chamber of Commerce, United States Fish and Wildlife Service, Daughters of the American Revolution, Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars of the Smithsonian Institution, United States Library of Congress and the United States Congress.

Dolly has rejected the Presidential Medal of Freedom twice. Another former president was shocked upon hearing the news, assuming she had received one already.

Dolly Parton grew up in an extremely impoverished area of the country. Her father was illiterate and access to medical care good medical care wasn't always available. She has been quiet about her philanthropy, but many of the ones that are publicly known have been focused on trying to help support her home state, particularly in the areas that she observed to be lacking - health and education.

Her biggest impact is probably the purchase and continued expansion of Dollywood, a themepark right in her home county. An assessment by the Tennessee Department of Transportation estimates the economic impact of Dollywood to be ~1.8 billion per year. Dollywood currently employs ~11,000 people. The impacts of a successful tourist attraction in an impoverished region cannot be overstated.

There are certainly many factors that may drive poverty rates, but census data appears to suggest that Sevier County performed better over the decades than surrounding counties, possibly due to the Dolly's repeated attempts to help the region, especially with the support of Dollywood.
